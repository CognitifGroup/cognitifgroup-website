The engine

Petalyx™

The instrument that takes the reading: a structured, six-axis measurement engine built to sound the terrain that Semantic Bathymetry™ describes.

Six axes, one instrument

Structured across six measurement dimensions.

Petalyx™ takes its reading across six independent axes, rendering each result as a petal — one for every dimension measured. Together the six petals form a single rosette: a compact, comparable signature of where a population sits on the semantic terrain, across every axis at once.

  • kAAxis 1 of 6
  • kBAxis 2 of 6
  • kCAxis 3 of 6
  • kDAxis 4 of 6
  • kEAxis 5 of 6
  • kFAxis 6 of 6
How it's delivered

Enterprise SaaS, delivered as aggregate signal.

Petalyx™ is built as a business-to-business subscription service. Enterprise clients receive aggregate, population-level readings across the six axes; no individual-level record is the deliverable. The underlying corpus is held to fixed minimum-population thresholds before any reading is released, so a result is only ever reported once enough independent responses stand behind it.

Input

A population, not a person

Petalyx™ is designed to measure population-scale associative patterns, not to profile any individual.

Output

A six-petal signature

Every reading is returned as a rosette across the six axes — comparable across time, cohort, and context.

Custody

Held apart, on purpose

Aggregate coordinates are held by Semba AI, an independent data custodian — see About Us.
