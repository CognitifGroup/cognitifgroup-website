The discipline

Semantic Bathymetry™

A method for measuring the shape of a population's associative meaning — where it is settled, and where it is still open water.

The metaphor, precisely

Attractors and diffuse spaces.

A language model's latent space is not flat. Some regions behave like mountains: a population's responses converge tightly around a single association, with low variance — we call these attractors. Other regions behave like open water: responses spread widely, with high variance and no dominant consensus — we call these diffuse spaces. Semantic Bathymetry™ is the discipline of surveying that terrain systematically, rather than assuming it is uniform.

Attractors

Low temperature

Where a population's associations are tightly bound: the same input reliably produces the same interpretation. These regions are stable, precise, and easy to trust as a fixed reading.

Diffuse spaces

High temperature

Where associations are loosely bound: the same input produces a wide spread of interpretation. These regions carry genuine ambiguity, and treating them as fixed points is itself a measurement error.

Why it matters

Most measurement treats the terrain as flat.

Conventional survey and sentiment tools take a single reading and report it as if the underlying terrain were uniform — as if every point in meaning-space had the same reliability. Semantic Bathymetry™ starts from the opposite assumption: reliability itself varies by location, and an organisation needs to know which of its readings sit on solid ground and which sit over open water before it acts on them.
